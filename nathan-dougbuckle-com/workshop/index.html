The strategic logic, in one line
What just shifted

The economics of design discovery just changed.

What used to take six weeks

A traditional Katapult kickoff was — and for most agencies still is — a two-day in-room workshop plus around four weeks of follow-on design work. Research, trends, customer voice, problem framing, concept exploration, sketches, industry positioning. We've charged $80K for it. Nathan said it's worth $25K. Both are true.

What an AI-augmented studio can now compress

The same depth of work — orchestrated through AI plus the best research tools the industry offers, with a senior designer intervening at six to eight critical moments — now fits inside a calendar week. The client commitment shrinks from days in a room to ten minutes on a portal.

The opportunity that opens

Pricing this at $4,000 looks aggressive against the $80K legacy number. It's intentional. The workshop isn't a revenue product — it's a trust-builder. We give clients the work they couldn't have produced themselves, before they commit to anything else, and let the trust do the rest.

Why this matters now

Other agencies will eventually build something like this. The window for being the first Australian agency known for it is short. Two-year horizon to build the brand association before "AI-augmented discovery workshop" becomes table stakes.

Introducing

The Workshop — the new Katapult front door.

The Workshop is a paid kickoff product, sold at $4,000, delivered in one week. The client (with two or three of their team) spends ten minutes each on a Katapult portal answering structured questions about their product, customer, category, and the strategic question keeping them up at night. That's the entire client commitment of time.

The week that follows: a senior Katapult designer orchestrates a simulated workshop. AI runs the synthesis at speed — research, macro trends, simulated customer voice, problem framing, concept divergence, sketches, ideation, industry positioning, business analysis. The senior intervenes 6–8 times across the week — 30 minutes each — to redirect, sharpen, choose, reframe, and shape the deliverable.

The client receives a polished design discovery report on day seven. Roughly one in four becomes a Katapult project — average size $80K. The workshop pays for itself; the project work is the prize.

How it works, in plain language

  1. The Workshop is the front door — every Katapult engagement starts here, deliberately under-priced so trust gets built before commitment.
  2. The client puts in ten minutes on a portal, with a few members of their team. Katapult puts in a week.

What's under the hood

Best of the industry, orchestrated by Katapult.

The $4K price point is only sustainable because Katapult orchestrates a capability stack a single client could never assemble. The client experiences one polished output. What they don't see is what's running underneath.

01

Macro & global trend intelligence

Katapult subscribes to industry-leading trend sources (Future Laboratory-tier) and amortises them across every workshop run. Each client gets a 3–5 year horizon on their category, woven into the output, without needing to know where it came from.

02

Simulated customer voice

Purpose-built AI personas, run to brief, producing real qualitative signal in days rather than weeks of recruitment. Best-in-class research capability brought in behind the scenes.

03

Cross-category pattern recognition

Twenty years of Katapult project history, structured for retrieval. The wellness-hardware client gets the kitchen-appliance pattern they'd otherwise never see.

04

Manufacturing reality, in the room

Katapult's curated supplier knowledge, certification timelines, materials cost benchmarks. The "is this actually buildable?" question gets answered in the workshop.

05

AI-augmented synthesis

The speed engine. Concept divergence, sketch generation, deck assembly, written narrative. AI does the hours of work in minutes; senior shapes what makes the cut.

06

The senior layer (the moat)

A Katapult designer with twenty years of pattern recognition, intervening 6–8 times across the week. The capabilities don't critique themselves. Seniors do.

Put together, this stack is what makes a $4K workshop sustainable while delivering $25K of value. No single client could subscribe to all of it. Katapult assembles it once, amortises it, and runs it across many workshops. That's the secret sauce.

The senior layer — the irreplaceable bit

Capabilities don't critique themselves. Seniors do.

The orchestrated stack is necessary but not sufficient. The thing that makes a Katapult workshop better than the same stack run by anyone else is the senior in the room — a designer who intervenes at every stage gate, redirects what AI got wrong, names what it got right, and shapes what the client takes home.

Ten things even the best AI stack still can't do without senior judgement:

  1. Knowing what to ask. AI generates infinite answers to wrong questions.
  2. Knowing when something is bullshit. Distinguishing the good answer from the merely plausible one.
  3. Connecting cross-category patterns. A wellness hardware client only knows wellness hardware; the senior has seen ten categories.
  4. Reading which way the room is leaning. The nervous laugh, the disagreement between CEO and head of product.
  5. Sequencing the work. AI generates everything at once. Humans know what matters first.
  6. Reaching for the right reference. Apple is obvious. Bellroy is specific to context.
  7. Reframing the brief. Client thinks they're building a wallet; should be building a wallet-shaped membership.
  8. Forcing convergence. AI defaults to more options. Humans know when to stop.
  9. Knowing what actually ships. Twenty years of production reality.
  10. The earned authority to say "this won't work." Trust that AI cannot have.

The senior is the principal. The stack is the instrument. Other agencies will build similar stacks; few have Katapult's senior bench to layer on top.

What could go wrong

The risks we're naming up front.

No hiding the obvious. Each risk with likelihood and the specific mitigation built into the model.

$4K reads as too cheap to be serious
Medium
Front the value story: "$25K of work for $4K." Show the report quality early. Position as a deliberate gift, not a commodity.
AI output stays generic without senior intervention
Medium
Six to eight 30-min senior checkpoints are non-negotiable. Refined prompts after each pilot. Quality bar enforced by the senior owning each workshop.
Conversion to project lower than projected
High initially
First-quarter conversion rate measured weekly. If below 15%, review the workshop-to-pitch handoff process. The $4K still funds the experiment.
Senior bandwidth eaten by workshops at the expense of projects
Medium
~3–4 hours per workshop. At 75/year, ~280 hours = under 10% of one senior's time. Scales fine. Train a second senior on the playbook by month 6.
Partner subscriptions cost more than projected
Low–Med
Subscriptions amortise across all workshops; the marginal cost per workshop is low. Cap discretionary partner spend at $20K/year for the first year.
Competing agencies build a similar offering
Medium over 18–24 months
