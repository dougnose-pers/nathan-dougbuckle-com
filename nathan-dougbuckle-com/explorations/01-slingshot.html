01 / Slingshot

A partner company.
Same DNA, different lane.

Capital-light. Brand-protected. Built to capture the part of the market Katapult cannot profitably serve today — and quietly feed the best ones back upstream.

The strongest commercial idea on the whole list. You raised the framing yourself in the May meeting — *"a member of your team that might be willing to take some equity, some ownership and really step up in responsibility... move into Katapult Ventures."* This is that, refined.

How it works, in plain language

Small company, big backing.
Built so everyone wins together.

  1. Nathan keeps control. Katapult is the largest shareholder and holds a brand-protective veto on anything that touches the parent name.
  2. A key person gets the chance to grow a company — internal first if the right candidate is already inside Katapult, external if not.
  3. They take meaningful equity in the success of the new venture.
  4. They draw a fallback salary at roughly 30–40% of what they'd otherwise be on. Enough to live; low enough to be entrepreneurial.
  5. The business pays the rest of their salary as revenue comes in — distributions scale with profitability.
  6. Together everyone grows it — and their stake in it — over time.
  7. 10% is reserved for advisors who help along the way (Doug, and potentially others as needed).

The detailed financials, equity splits, capital flows and 3-year P&L sit in the Slingshot pitch page. This page is for the spirit; the pitch deck is for the spreadsheet.

Why it was interesting

Three reasons it kept coming up.

First — it's defensive. AI is going to commoditise the lower end of physical product design in the next 12–24 months. If Katapult doesn't capture that segment with its own AI-first offering, someone else will. The "if you're not doing it, others will" position is real, not rhetorical.

Second — it's offensive. Katapult currently turns away or scares off founders with sub-$50K budgets every month. They build with someone worse, or they don't build at all. A meaningful slice of those conversations could land in a productised offer that meets them where they are — and feed the best ones back upstream into Katapult main as referral-fee revenue.

Third — it solves your bandwidth problem. You said it three different ways in our meeting. Slingshot externalises the operational load to someone else, with you protecting the brand through a golden-share veto rather than active control.

What the customer sees

A clean website with transparent tiered pricing — $5K Discovery Sprint, $14K Concept Exploration, $28K Design-Ready Brief — plus a modular menu of add-ons. AI-augmented delivery. Faster turnaround than traditional agencies. Katapult senior judgement layered on every output through stage-gate critique. The whole thing reads as *the small, fast version of Katapult* — explicit partner relationship, explicit upgrade path.

What's been built

A team-ready pitch deck has been built and sits at its own page — single-scroll, presentable in a meeting, designed for you to walk Greta and Mick through. The whole financial picture, governance, cap table, build sequence and risks are all in there. Worth opening before any conversation with the team.
