04 / The toolkit

Eight tools.
Productised, repeatable, attachable.

Each one is a deliverable Katapult is already producing inside larger engagements today. Productising surfaces them, prices them transparently, and lets account leads sell them as discrete line items.

The unifying frame: *"Here's a complete design engagement — and here are six other things we can do for you while we're already in the file."* Each tool has its own AI harness, its own template, its own senior touch budget. Each one's marginal cost approaches zero. Each one is real existing work, packaged.

Where we landed

A hit-list of eight. $2K–$5K each. Katapult main only.

Not on the public katapult.com.au pricing page (that would pressure the premium brand). Not on Slingshot either, at least at first (Slingshot needs to launch on its own merits before becoming a delivery channel for these). Sold by Katapult account leads inside existing engagements, listed as optional menu items in proposals or surfaced mid-project as the obvious next step.

Why it was interesting

The most boring of the five.
That's why it should ship first.

This is the one idea with no entity to stand up, no new sales motion to build, no public brand to launch. It's the discipline of writing down what Katapult is already doing and putting a price next to each line. Done well, it lifts average project value by $5K–$10K per client without anyone selling harder.

Build sequence is three waves over nine months:

  • Wave 1 (months 1–3) — Brand stack + most-asked. Brand Identity Sprint, Pitch Deck, Naming with Clearance. Highest AI maturity. Test the production model with the easiest three.
  • Wave 2 (months 4–6) — Validate-and-protect. Simulated Customer Interviews, Patent / Prior-Art Search, Compliance Roadmap. Used during development.
  • Wave 3 (months 7–9) — Ship-to-market. Factory Onboarding Pack, Kickstarter Handover. Most production-side; built last when the harness is well-calibrated.

Senior owns each tool's quality bar. Account leads sell. AI does 60–70% of the drafting work. Senior layers the judgement on top — 30–120 minutes per deliverable depending on the tool. That ratio holds the 93% gross margin.

One forward-looking note

Once Slingshot is operational, every one of these tools could also be offered through its public menu — same templates, same AI harness, same senior review coming from Katapult under the inter-company services arrangement. One harness, two delivery channels. Captured as a v2 move; doesn't block anything now.
